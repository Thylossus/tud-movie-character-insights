Openness
Openness, or Open to Experience, is the extent to which a person is open to experiencing a variety of activities.
Neuroticism
Neuroticism, also referred to as Emotional Range or Natural Reactions, is the extent to which a person's emotions are sensitive to the individual's environment.
Conscientiousness
Conscientiousness ("Gewissenhaftigkeit") is a person's tendency to act in an organized or thoughtful way.
Agreeableness
Agreeableness ("Verträglichkeit") is a person's tendency to be compassionate and cooperative toward others.
Extraversion
Extraversion is a person's tendency to seek stimulation in the company of others.
Altruism
Find that helping others is genuinely rewarding, that doing things for others is a form of self-fulfillment rather than self-sacrifice. ("Nächstenliebe")
Assertiveness
Like to take charge and direct the activities of others. They tend to be leaders in groups. ("Durchsetzungskraft")
Depression
Tend to react more readily to life's ups and downs.
Cheerfulness
Experience a range of positive feelings, including happiness, enthusiasm, optimism, and joy.("Fröhlichkeit")
Imagination
View the real world as often too plain and ordinary. They use fantasy not as an escape but as a way of creating for themselves a richer and more interesting inner-world. ("Fantasie")
Intellect
Are intellectually curious and tend to think in symbols and abstractions.
Adventurousness
Are eager to try new activities and experience different things. They find familiarity and routine boring.("Abenteuerlust")
Achievement striving
Try hard to achieve excellence. Their drive to be recognized as successful keeps them on track as they work hard to accomplish their goals. ("Erfolgsstreben")
Self-discipline
Have the self-discipline, or "will-power," to persist at difficult or unpleasant tasks until they are completed.
Liberalism
Have a readiness to challenge authority, convention, and traditional values.
